Panjab

The Golden City

Punjab is a northern state of India, known for its rich cultural heritage, agricultural prosperity, and vibrant traditions. The name Punjab means "Land of Five Rivers" (referring to the Beas, Ravi, Sutlej, Chenab, and Jhelum). It has played a crucial role in India's history, particularly in the Sikh religion and the freedom struggle.

    History

    Punjab has a rich history, from the Indus Valley Civilization to the Sikh Empire. It saw invasions by Persians, Greeks, Mughals, and British rule. The Sikh Gurus and Maharaja Ranjit Singh shaped its identity. Partition in 1947 divided Punjab, but it remains a vibrant cultural and economic hub today......

    cultural of Punjab

    Punjab has a rich and vibrant culture deeply rooted in tradition, music, dance, and cuisine. Known as the land of five rivers, it is famous for its lively festivals, colorful attire, and warm hospitality. The people of Punjab, called Punjabis, are known for their exuberance, bravery, and strong sense of community. Bhangra and Giddha, energetic folk dances, are central to Punjabi celebrations, often performed during festivals like Baisakhi and Lohri. The music, featuring instruments like the dhol and tumbi, reflects the regionâ€™s enthusiasm and spirit. Punjabi cuisine is another highlight, with mouthwatering dishes like Makki di Roti, Sarson da Saag, Butter Chicken, and Lassi. The tradition of langar (community kitchen) in Sikh temples symbolizes selfless service and equality. The culture is also deeply influenced by Sikhism, with the Golden Temple in Amritsar being a spiritual and cultural center. Punjabi literature, folklore, and crafts, such as Phulkari embroidery, further showcase its rich heritage. Despite modern influences, Punjab retains its traditional values of hard work, resilience, and joy. Its culture continues to thrive, blending history with contemporary vibrancy.

    Bhangra:Bhangra and Giddha are forms of dance and music that originated in the Punjab region.


    Bhangra dance began as a folk dance conducted by Punjabi farmers to celebrate the coming of the harvest season. The specific moves of Bhangra reflect the manner in which villagers farmed their land. This hybrid dance became Bhangra. The folk dance has been popularised in the western world by Punjabis in England, Canada and the US where competitions are held.[242] It is seen in the West as an expression of South Asian culture as a whole

    Punjabi folklore:The folk heritage of the Punjab reflects its thousands of years of history.


    While Majhi is considered to be the standard dialect of Punjabi language, there are a number of Punjabi dialects through which the people communicate.. These include Malwai, Doabi and Puadhi. The songs, ballads, epics and romances are generally written and sung in these dialects
